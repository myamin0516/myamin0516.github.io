Visualization Overview

This project aims to visualize median house prices across the U.S. for 2023 to 2024. The data was scraped from realtor.com by Ahmed Shahriar Sakib and last updated in March 2024. It was merged with a comprehensive city dataset that includes county names, providing a clearer picture of housing trends nationwide. The choropleth is a nice way view data, however it's surface level and hard to draw concrete insights from. This visualization a precursor to the region vs region analysis.

Scope and Goals

The primary goal is to understand house prices around the U.S. before doing a deeper dive.

  • Visualize median house prices: Create a choropleth of average U.S. house prices by county
  • Identify trends: Are there any genral patterns we can take to the next step of analysis?
  • Ask questions:
    • Why does the West appear to have more expensive housing?
    • Why does the Midwest and Northeast have cheaper housing?
    • Is being near the coast a large factor in house price?
    • What other factors are influencing house price besides geographic location?
